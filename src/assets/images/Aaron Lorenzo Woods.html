About Me

Education and Research

I'm currently a doctoral student in a functional optics lab. My work revolves around developing cutting-edge imaging technologies to explore the brain's intricate structures. Specifically, I'm using Multiphoton Microscopy and Laser Speckle Contrast Imaging to understand neurovascular physiology and the impact of diseases like stroke. The end goal? To unravel the brain's mysteries using a blend of these advanced imaging techniques and computational analysis. Although I'm keen to delve into studying consciousness, my current focus is on mastering brain imaging.

+

Interests

Outside the lab, I have a creative side. Music is a big part of my life, and I love playing the ukulele and guitar. Creating songs allows me to express myself in a different medium. Also, I enjoy painting, particularly abstract and contemporary art, which lets me explore and express different perspectives. On the technical side, I'm intrigued by machine learning and its potential applications in image processing.

+

Future Goals

Looking ahead, I have three main aspirations. I want to earn my Ph.D., delving further into the world of brain imaging and machine learning. In my free time, I plan to craft an album, channeling my creativity and passion for music. And down the line, I'm open to exploring entrepreneurial opportunities where I can apply my expertise to create real-world impact.
